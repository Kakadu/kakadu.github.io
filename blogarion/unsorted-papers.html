CORRECTNESS OF UNIFICATION WITHOUT OCCUR CHECK IN PROLOG (Journal of Logic Programming, 1994)

Occurs check make unification to work much slowly but without it unification may lead to wrong answers. Folks invent a way to preprocess Prolog programs to detect which terms can be recursive and use occurs-check unification only for this terms.

Some prolog implementations seems be able to deal with terms with cycles without cyclicing the programm. Some dirty hack probably....
